Raw BB84 Quantum Transmission

(responder's side)


The raw BB84 quantum transmission generates the raw material from which a secret-key will be distilled. It is a physical implementation of the BB84 coding scheme.

Rhough Description

A raw key is a sequence of classical bits from the which the final secret key should be distilled. A raw BB84 key is a sequence of transmitted pulses that have have been detected on the responder side. The pulses are prepared according to the BB84 quantum coding scheme:
  • For each pulse i, the bit bi to be transmitted is chosen randomly by the sender,
  • A transmission basis ti is chosen randomly among the rectilinear "+" and diagonal "X" bases,
  • A qubit in quantum state |bi>ti is sent to the receiver,
  • For each pulse, the receiver measures the qubit in a random basis t'i (``+'' or ``X'') and records the classical one-bit outcome b'i.

The raw key is defined as the sequence b=b1,...,bn on the sender's side and b'=b'1,...,b'n on the receiver's side for the detected pulses. In an ideal situation, the quantum transmission has the following property:
  • For all pulses i such that ti=t'i we have bi=b'i.
However, the quantum trasmission is not perfect and errors occur even when the basis ti=t'i. More information on the physical implementation of the BB84 coding scheme can be found here.
