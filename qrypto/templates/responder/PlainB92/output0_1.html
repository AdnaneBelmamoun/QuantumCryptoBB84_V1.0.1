Plain B92 Quantum Transmission

(responder's side)

This protocol generates a key between the sender and the receiver that is in most positions identical. After this phase, both players share a similar key that is partially secret and for which they have en estimate of the error-rate. This is called the plain key.

Raw B92 Quantum Transmission

The first step of a Plain B92 quantum transmission is the B92 raw key generation. Details about this BB84 raw key generation can be found there:
  • RawB92
The raw key generation allows Bob and Alice to weakly agree on a sequence of bits. Both sequences differ in many positions even in the case of error-free quantum communication. The next phase of the protocol allows to keep only the positions for which the same bit is shared except for possible errors caused by noisy quantum transmission.

Keeping Good Positions

At this stage, the parties agree upon the positions of the raw B92 quantum transmission for which qubits have been sent and received correctly. Those positions correspond to the pulses for which the receiver has identified the state sent. These positions are the one that are not indicated by ? or ?.
