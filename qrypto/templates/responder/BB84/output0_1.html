BB84 Secret-Key Distribution

(responder's side)


From a reconciled BB84 key, Alice and Bob obtain their final secret key by applying privacy amplification. The resulting secret-key can then be used to encrypt private messages to be sent over an insecure channel.

Reconciled BB84 Key

A reconciled BB84 key is a key shared by Alice and Bob but not perfectly secret. An eavesdropper may obtain information about the key by:
  • Eavesdropping the quantum channel during quantum transmission and/or
  • Listening the public discussion taking place during reconciliation and confirmation.
A reconciled key of size 610 has been generated during reconciliation:

A total of 128 parity bits were revealed during reconciliation and 10 were revealed during confirmation. Next, privacy amplification is applied in order to remove the eavesdropper's information about the reconciled key. The total amount of leaked information is d= 138 parity bits.

The Final Secret-Key Obtained From Privacy Amplification

Let R be the reconciled key of length m. Let d be the number of parity bits disclosed during reconciliation and confirmation. Privacy amplification is the process of applying a random and publicly known hashing function f:{0,1}m->{0,1}m-d-s upon the reconciled key. The parameter s is a security parameter which, as it grows, results in a more secret final key S. The final secret-key is then simply computed as S=f (R). For this execution we have,
  • m= 610 ,
  • d= 138 ,
  • s= 0.05 *m= 31 .
A randomly selected hashing function f:{0,1} 610 ->{0,1} 441 is applied privately by Bob and Alice thus producing the final secret-key f (R):
