Plain BB84 Quantum Transmission

(initiator's side)


This protocol generates a key between the sender and the receiver that is in most positions identical. After this phase, both players share a similar key that is partially secret. This is called the plain key.

Raw BB84 Key Generation

The first step of a Plain BB84 quantum transmission is the BB84 raw key generation. Details about this BB84 raw key generation can be found in:
  • RawBB84

The raw key generation, Alice and Bob to weakly agree on a sequence of bits. Both sequences differ in many positions (about 1/2) even in the case of error-free quantum communication. The next phase of the protocol allows to keep only the positions for which the same bit is shared except for possible errors caused by noisy quantum transmission.

Keeping good positions


The good positions are the one for which the basis used to measure the qubit is the same as the one used to send the qubit. In this case the sender and the receiver should end up (if no transmission error occured) with the same classical bit. Alice and Bob determine which positions have matching bases by announcing the sequence of bases over the public channel. For this execution the matching positions appear like that:

From the original 1500 qubits sent and successfully received between Alice and Bob , 746 classical bits are kept. The kept positions are then sampled in order to find an estimate for the error-rate of the quantum transmission.

Statistics

Here are some statistics about this execution:
  • The execution started with a raw key of 1500 qubits.
  • The annoucement of the bases has revealed 746 positions that are likely to match.
  • The sample of 150 random positions has revealed:
    • 136 different positions,
    • The random sampling found 5 errors.
    • The observed error-rate is 0.033333335 .
    • The estimate is within 0.059499145 with probability larger than 95 %.
  • The final plain key is 610 bits long after removing the sampled positions.
